## Beyond Identity Labels

In academic discourse, sexual orientation is more than just a label; it is a complex social construct.

- The Split Attraction Model (SAM): This framework allows us to differentiate between "romantic" and "sexual" pulls. One can be aromantic but sexual, or biromantic but asexual.
- Heteronormativity: The social belief that heterosexuality is the default or "normal" state. This often leads to the erasure or marginalization of non-straight identities.
- Conceptual Clarity: It is vital to remember that who you are (identity) and who you love (orientation) are distinct and independent variables.

## Closing Reflection

Sexual orientation is not merely a personal preference; it is socially interpreted, regulated, and politicized. Understanding attraction academically allows us to reduce harm and erasure. Desire exists within society; to understand desire, we must first understand power.
