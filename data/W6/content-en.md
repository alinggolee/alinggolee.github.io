## Parenting as Social Regulation

Parenting is often seen as a personal choice. But in fact, it is strongly influenced by society, social systems, and cultural expectations.

Heteronormativity and Social Rules:
 Society usually has an idea of what a “normal family” looks like. There are also unwritten gender rules. For example, people may believe that “good parents” should raise “normal” children in a certain way. These social expectations can sometimes be stronger than the law. They guide, control, and judge people’s behavior.

## The Cycle of Oppression:
 We will use the “Cycle of Oppression” model (stereotypes → prejudice → discrimination → oppression → internalized oppression) to understand how society creates pressure on non-traditional families.

  - Think of a common "moral panic" regarding diverse families (e.g., "the child will be confused"). If we replace this fear with a vision of safety and support, what is one "small win" or action we can implement in our schools today?
  
## Closing Reflection

Parenting is a social practice. When diversity is treated as a problem, families carry the heavy burden of judgment. Our role is to shift the perspective from judgment to support, and from structure to action.
