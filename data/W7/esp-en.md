## Before TEEMI: Understanding "Gender Privilege"

**Part 1: Reading**
What is Gender Privilege?

Gender privilege means that some people have advantages because of their gender.

For example:

1️⃣ Workplace
In some companies, men are promoted more often than women.
Even when women have the same skills, they may be questioned more.

2️⃣ Public Safety
In many places, men can walk alone at night without much fear.
Women often think about their safety and worry about harassment.

3️⃣ Speaking in Public
Men are often seen as confident leaders.
Women who speak strongly may be called “too emotional” or “too aggressive.”

These examples show how gender privilege works in daily life.

**Part 2: Real Story – Paula Stone Williams**

Paula Stone Williams was once a successful male pastor.
Later in life, she transitioned and became a woman.

After her transition, she noticed big changes:

People interrupted her more.

Her ideas were not taken seriously.

She received less respect in meetings.

She said that she lost male privilege.

Her story helps us understand how gender privilege works in society.

**Part 3: Discussion Questions**

What is gender privilege?

What changes did Paula experience after her transition?

Do you think gender privilege exists in Taiwan? Why or why not?

Can you give one example of gender privilege in your workplace or school?
