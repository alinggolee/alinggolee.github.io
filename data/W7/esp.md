## Before TEEMI: Understanding "Gender Privilege (性別特權)"

## Part 1: Reading (閱讀)
What is Gender Privilege (性別特權)?

Gender privilege (性別特權) means that some people have advantages (優勢) because of their gender (性別).

For example:

1.Workplace (職場)
In some companies (公司), men are promoted (升遷) more often than women.
Even when women have the same skills (技能), they may be questioned (被質疑) more.

2.Public Safety (公共安全)
In many places (地方), men can walk alone at night without much fear (恐懼).
Women often think about their safety (安全) and worry about harassment (騷擾).

3.Speaking in Public (公開發言)
Men are often seen as confident (自信的) leaders (領導者).
Women who speak strongly may be called “too emotional (太情緒化)” or “too aggressive (太強勢).”

These examples show how gender privilege (性別特權) works in daily life (日常生活).

## Part 2: Real Story – Paula Stone Williams

Paula Stone Williams was once a successful (成功的) male pastor (男性牧師).
Later in life, she transitioned (跨性別轉換) and became a woman.

After her transition (轉換), she noticed big changes (重大改變):

People interrupted (打斷) her more.

Her ideas (想法) were not taken seriously (未被認真看待).

She received less respect (較少尊重) in meetings (會議).

She said that she lost male privilege (男性特權).

Her story (故事) helps us understand (理解) how gender privilege (性別特權) works in society (社會).
